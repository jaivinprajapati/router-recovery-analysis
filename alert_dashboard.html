1. Churned Customers Without PUT Created

i
Source: T_ROUTER_USER_MAPPING (recharge expiry) + DYNAMODB_READ.TASKS (PUT tickets) + T_DEVICE_AUDIT (device still at same NAS)
Logic: Customer recharge expired 15+ days ago, no subsequent recharge, router still at same customer location, but no PUT ticket created
Date: Expected PUT creation date = expiry + 16 days
Aligned with: Metabase dashboard #1143, card 10399
Daily count of customers who churned but no PUT ticket was raised.
Why this matters: This is the entry point of the entire recovery funnel. If a customer churns and no PUT is created, the device is silently lost — no partner action, no RA fallback. Every missed PUT = a device Wiom has zero chance of recovering.

2. Daily PUT Tickets Assigned

i
Source: PROD_DB.PUBLIC.TASK_LOGS
Filter: event_name = 'ASSIGN_TICKET_ROUTER_PICKUP'
Date: DATEADD(minute,330,added_time) — IST
Benchmark: Nov-Dec avg ~67% assigned within 24hrs, median 11h. Assignment rate has been declining (80% → 66% Nov→Mar).
Count of PUT tickets assigned to a partner/agent each day.
Why this matters: Assignment is the handoff from system to field. Tickets created but never assigned = dead tickets. A drop in assignments while creation stays flat signals a dispatch bottleneck — tickets pile up unactioned.

3. Success / Total Closed

i
DOD/WOW: TASK_PERFORMANCE (resolution date) LEFT JOIN TASKS (deduped). Successfully Closed = TASKS.STATUS=2. Denominator = all resolved that day.
MOM: TASKS only (deduped, rn=1). Creation month basis. 21-day rule applied (only tickets created ≥21 days ago).
WOW: 7-day trailing sum(success) / sum(total closed).
Of all PUT tickets closed, what % were successfully closed (STATUS=2).
Why this matters: The core health metric. Tells you whether the field is actually recovering devices. A sustained drop means something fundamental is failing — partner effort, customer cooperation, or process.

4. Open Ticket Aging

i
Table: DYNAMODB_READ.TASKS (deduped, rn=1)
Filter: TYPE='ROUTER_PICKUP', STATUS IN (0,1), CREATED ≥ 2025-11-01
Buckets: 0-7d (healthy), 8-14d (aging), 15-21d (danger), 21+d (SLA breach)
Currently open PUT tickets bucketed by age.
Why this matters: Open tickets are future failures. A ticket at 18 days old will almost certainly fail. Rising 15-21d count = failure spike incoming next week.

5. Failed / Total Closed

i
Same source as Metric 3 (complement). Failed = TASKS.STATUS=3 (send_to_wiom).
This is the top-of-funnel for the RA team.
Of all PUT tickets closed, what % were unsuccessfully closed (STATUS=3).
Why this matters: Every failed ticket becomes RA's problem. A spike here means RA team needs to brace for volume. Also flags if partners are bulk-closing tickets as failed without recovery attempt.

6. RA Recovery Efficiency

i
Failed tickets: TASKS (STATUS=3) + TASK_PERFORMANCE (CUSTOMER_DEVICE_ID, resolved time)
RA returns: T_DEVICE_AUDIT where extra_data.returnedBy='RA' and router_state='returned_in_office'
Join: CUSTOMER_DEVICE_ID = DEVICE_ID
Aging: days since PUT failed, bucketed. "Still pending" = device not yet returned by RA.
After partner fails a PUT ticket, is RA picking up the device? Aging view of failed tickets by RA return status.
Why this matters: RA is the last line of defense. When partners fail, RA picks up the device. A failed PUT with no subsequent RA return = device permanently lost. Rising "still pending" count in older buckets = RA falling behind or devices slipping through.
